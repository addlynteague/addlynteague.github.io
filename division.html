FSU Division of Student Affairs

Web Intern

Florida State University’s Division of Student Affairs is a network of campus departments and programs that offer resources to help make a student’s experience positive and impactful. At the time (Fall 2017) the school’s digital presence was going through a rebranding process. All of the websites the Division of Student Affairs controlled were to be switched over to its new template on the content management system, Drupal. The website I had the pleasure of switching over was the Oglesby Union!

This task took all semester. The Union’s website composed of eight tabs, around 28 sub-tabs, and many more links, files, pictures, and tabs underneath the sub-tabs. As a Web Intern, I would create pages on Drupal, organize them under one another, link pages if needed, copy and paste text, edit the links and files in HTML, download media to the system, and place content in their respected blocks. My past experience with a web design course and using HTML helped me tremendously when I needed to space pictures, create tables, and edit links.

The top three pictures are snapshots of what the former Oglesby Union website looked like, including two pictures of the Home page and the History page. The middle row is of the same two pages, but rebranded using Drupal. The last row consists of two extra snapshots of pages on the website. Each include a side tab bar. They also feature a table, Contact block, hyperlinks, and links to forms.
